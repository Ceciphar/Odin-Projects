Ghanaian Fried Plantains

Description

Ghanaian fried plantains are a delightful and versatile dish that can be enjoyed by themselves or as a complement to other flavors. The crispy exterior and soft, sweet interior of the plantains make them a favorite snack or side dish in Ghanaian cuisine.

Ingredients

  • Ripe plantains (2-3, peeled)
  • Vegetable oil (for frying)
  • Salt

Steps

  1. Slice the peeled plantains into diagonal pieces, approximately ½ inch thick. You can cut them straight or at an angle, depending on your preference.
  2. In a large skillet or frying pan, pour enough vegetable oil to cover the bottom of the pan and heat it over medium heat until hot. You can test the oil's temperature by dropping a small piece of plantain into the oil – if it sizzles and starts to fry, the oil is ready.
  3. Carefully place the plantain slices into the hot oil, ensuring they are in a single layer and not overcrowded. Fry the plantains for about 2-3 minutes on each side or until they turn golden brown. Use a slotted spoon or tongs to flip the plantains halfway through the cooking process.
  4. Once the plantains are golden brown and crispy, remove them from the oil and place them on a plate lined with paper towels to drain excess oil.
  5. Optional: Sprinkle a pinch of salt over the fried plantains for a savory touch. Ghanaian fried plantains can be enjoyed both sweet and savory, so adjust the salt according to your taste preference.
  6. Serve the hot fried plantains immediately as a snack, side dish, or part of a main course. They pair well with a variety of dishes, such as grilled meats, stews, or even on their own as a delicious and satisfying treat.
