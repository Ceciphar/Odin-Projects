Fried Rice

Description

Pork fried rice is a popular Chinese dish that combines tender pieces of pork, cooked rice, and an assortment of vegetables and seasonings. It starts with crispy stir-fried rice mixed with marinated pork, adding savory and slightly sweet flavors. Vegetables like carrots, peas, and onions are added for color and texture, cooked briefly to maintain their crispness. Soy sauce, oyster sauce, garlic, ginger, and sesame oil enhance the dish with umami taste and aroma. Garnished with green onions, this versatile dish can be enjoyed as a main course or side dish, customizable with other ingredients like shrimp, chicken, or beef, or made vegetarian with tofu or extra vegetables. Pork fried rice offers a flavorful and satisfying meal, combining the goodness of rice, tender pork, vibrant vegetables, and a delightful blend of seasonings.

Ingredients

  • 1 tablespoon butter
  • 1 (6 ounce) boneless pork loin chop, cut into small pieces
  • 1 green onion, chopped
  • 1/4 cup chopped carrot
  • 1/4 cup chopped broccoli
  • 1 egg, beaten
  • 1 cup cold cooked rice
  • 1/4 cup frozen peas
  • 1 1/2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1/8 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1/8 teaspoon ground ginger

Steps

  1. Melt butter in a large non-stick skillet over medium heat.
  2. Add pork, green onion, carrot, and broccoli;
  3. cook and stir until pork is cooked through, 7 to 10 minutes.
  4. Transfer pork mixture to a bowl and return skillet to medium heat.
  5. Stir egg into the skillet and scramble until completely set.
  6. Add pork mixture back into the skillet;
  7. stir in rice, peas, soy sauce, garlic powder, and ground ginger.
  8. Cook and stir until heated through, 7 to 10 minutes.
